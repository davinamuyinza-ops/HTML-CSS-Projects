Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist for girls' education

Malala Yousafzai is the youngest person to receive the Nobel Peace Prize at age 17. She began advocating for girls to have the right to go to school under Taliban rule. After surviving a violent attack in 2012, her cause gained global attention. She co-founded the Malala Fund, wrote the memoir I Am Malala, and continues to champion education for all.

Time-Line of Achievements

  • 1997 – Born in Mingora, Swat Valley, Pakistan.
  • 2008 – At age 11, begins speaking publicly about girls’ education rights.
  • 2009 – Writes anonymous blog for BBC Urdu about life under the Taliban.
  • 2011 – Nominated for the International Children's Peace Prize.
  • 2012 – Survives an assassination attempt by the Taliban.
  • 2013 – Publishes memoir I Am Malala. Speaks at the United Nations.
  • 2014 – Wins the Nobel Peace Prize at age 17 (youngest ever).
  • 2017 – Becomes UN Messenger of Peace.
  • 2020 – Graduates from the University of Oxford with a degree in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics.
  • 2021 – Signs deal with Apple TV+ to produce content highlighting women and children.
  • Present – Continues global advocacy through the Malala Fund for girls’ education.
